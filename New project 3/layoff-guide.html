Layoff Survival Guide

Navigate the uncertainty with a little more steadiness.

Being laid off can shake your confidence, routine, and sense of stability. This guide is here to offer practical support without judgment or pressure.

1

Start with the first 48 hours

  • Pause before making big decisions. It is okay if your first priority is just getting through the shock.
  • Save important documents like severance details, benefits information, and recent pay records.
  • Write down immediate questions for HR about healthcare, final pay, references, and access to work samples if relevant.
2

Protect your energy while you regroup

  • Keep a small daily structure: wake up, eat, move a little, and choose one job-search task instead of trying to do everything.
  • Tell one or two trusted people what happened so you are not carrying it alone.
  • Limit comparison if it starts making the situation feel worse. A layoff often says more about the company than about your worth.
3

Focus on practical next steps

  • Update your resume and LinkedIn with your strongest recent work while the details are still fresh.
  • Make a short target list of roles you could realistically apply to in the next 2 weeks.
  • Reach out to one person at a time for support, insight, or leads instead of trying to network all at once.
4

What to remember

This is a hard experience, and it is completely normal if your confidence feels shaky for a while. You do not need to turn the next few days into a perfect recovery plan. A calmer approach is often more sustainable: protect your basics, gather information, and take one thoughtful step at a time.
